About Earth2.0

Earth2.0 is a game about climate change. It will function as a user interface for a simple climate-driven population model. It will be fun also (hopefully).

You play by taking the big and small decisions that make or break for a population living in a treachorous model earth.

Your objective is to guide the whole system into an equilibrium by managing a dynamic population, and the environment on which it depends. Good luck!

Why Population?

Human populations do not stand still. Population models can help us to explore the impacts of our actions for present and future generations.

Why Games?

Games on the subject of climate change are well-suited to address challenges of scientific communication and engagement; we learn when we play.

Game mechanics

Even a simple set of rules can give rise to complex behaviours. John Conway's game of life (example below) uses four rules to decide whether a cell lives or dies in over time. Earth2.0 will assign rules to model individuals.


Your population has basic requirements for land, water and food. But they can only tolerate a particular set of environmental conditions. These conditions affect how they move, multiply and die.


The land that this population lives in is not static either. Basic rules simulate the movement of heat and energy to determine climate and vegetation. And a population can alter its environment through consumption, development and pollution.
